So you want to eat more plants.

No guilt. No pressure. Just five practical tips to help you get started.

Click a tip to expand it.

Take it at your own pace — every tip is a step forward.

Instead of removing things from your diet, replace them. Oat milk instead of dairy. Mushrooms instead of ground beef. Chickpeas instead of chicken in your curry. Small swaps add up without feeling like deprivation — and most of the time, you won't even miss the original.

The number one reason plant-based food gets a bad rap is under-seasoning — not the plants themselves. Garlic, cumin, smoked paprika, soy sauce, nutritional yeast, lemon juice, and chili flakes are your new best friends. Be bold. Season like you mean it.

Keep lentils, canned chickpeas, black beans, oats, canned tomatoes, pasta, rice, and nuts on hand at all times. With a stocked pantry like this, you're always 20 minutes away from a solid, filling plant-based meal — even when you haven't planned ahead.

Indian, Ethiopian, Mexican, Middle Eastern, and Thai food all have incredible plant-based dishes that have existed for centuries — long before "vegan" was even a word. You're not inventing something new. You're discovering something that's been delicious all along.

Eating plant-based 70% of the time is a real win. Don't let chasing a perfect label get in the way of real progress. Every single plant-based meal counts. There's no score to keep and no one grading you. Just more plants, more often — that's the whole goal.

Easy swaps to try this week.

Simple 1-for-1 replacements that most people don't even notice.

Instead of... Try this 🌱
Cow's milk Oat milk or almond milk
Ground beef Lentils or walnut-mushroom mix
Chicken in curry Chickpeas or tofu
Butter for cooking Olive oil or coconut oil
Beef tacos Black bean or jackfruit tacos
Scrambled eggs Scrambled tofu with turmeric
