8 Vegan Myths. Busted.

Click any card to flip it and reveal the truth.

0 of 8 myths busted
01 MYTH

"You can't get enough protein on a vegan diet."

click to flip →
BUSTED 💥

Plants are packed with protein.

Lentils, chickpeas, tofu, tempeh, edamame, and quinoa are all high in protein. Most people easily hit their daily protein needs on a plant-based diet. Many elite athletes compete on plants.

02 MYTH

"Eating vegan is way too expensive."

click to flip →
BUSTED 💥

Beans and rice don't break the bank.

Legumes, grains, oats, and frozen vegetables are some of the cheapest foods available. Studies show vegan diets can cost up to 40% less than meat-based diets.

03 MYTH

"Vegan food is bland and boring."

click to flip →
BUSTED 💥

Have you tried it though?

Ethiopian, Indian, Thai, Mexican, and Middle Eastern cuisines are all heavily plant-based and incredibly flavorful. If your vegan food is bland, the problem is the seasoning — not the plants.

04 MYTH

"You'll be hungry all the time."

click to flip →
BUSTED 💥

Fiber keeps you full for hours.

Plant foods are high in fiber, which slows digestion and keeps you satisfied. A big bowl of lentil soup or a chickpea stew is genuinely filling. The key is eating enough — not just salads.

05 MYTH

"Vegans are unhealthy — they're missing nutrients."

click to flip →
BUSTED 💥

Plan well and you're covered.

The American Dietetic Association says a well-planned vegan diet meets all nutritional needs. B12 is worth supplementing, but whole plant foods are loaded with vitamins and minerals meat lacks.

06 MYTH

"You have to give up all your favorite foods."

click to flip →
BUSTED 💥

There's a plant-based version of everything.

Pizza, burgers, ice cream, mac and cheese — plant-based versions of comfort food exist and most of them genuinely slap. You're not giving things up, you're discovering new versions.

07 MYTH

"Going vegan is all-or-nothing."

click to flip →
BUSTED 💥

Progress beats perfection every time.

Eating plant-based 5 days a week still makes a real difference. Going 80% plant-based is infinitely better than not trying at all. Start anywhere — the goal is more plants, not a perfect label.

08 MYTH

"One person's diet doesn't make a difference."

click to flip →
BUSTED 💥

The math says otherwise.

Going vegan for one year saves roughly 401,500 gallons of water, 14,600 sq ft of forest, and 7,300 lbs of CO₂. Individual choices multiplied across millions of people reshape entire industries.
